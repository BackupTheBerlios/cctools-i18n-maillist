> one more thing. there's a long string in p6.po that mentions public
> domain dedication. in some jurisdiction, in poland for instance, the
> issue with public domain is complicated due to the irrevocability of
> moral rights - so it might not be wise to include such a simple
> statement on this issue... unfortunately i do not have a good idea how
> to describe it differently.

Good point; I know that the Public Domain dedication
(http://creativecommons.org/licenses/publicdomain) is based on US law
and that other jurisdictions approach this differently.  Unfortunately
the web services do not provide a way to filter license classes (and
even if they did, its conceivable that someone running ccPublisher in a
different locale would be licensing work protected by US copyright law).

For the record, the text in question reads:

"With a Creative Commons license, you keep your copyright but allow
people to copy and distribute your work provided the give you credit --
and only on the conditions you specify here.  If you want to offer your
work with no conditions, choose the Public Domain."

I'm open to suggestions on how to rephrase or translate this
appropriately...
