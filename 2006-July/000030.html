It's a bug.  Something that needs to be done before the 2.4 release is a
normalization of the strings.  I've opened an issue for that in the
tracker (http://roundup.creativecommons.org/ccpublisher/issue43).

> 
> 2) Maybe its just me, but some of the English text is kinda cryptic or
> 'doesn't feel right'. Such as:
> 
> - "That identifier does not conform to the Internet Archive's naming
> standards."
> 
> Shouldn't that be changed to this?
> 
> - "That identifier is not available."

The problem is that the first message is actually more accurate -- it
doesn't matter if identifiers which contain spaces, are less than 5
characters or more than 100 characters are available are not -- they
don't meet IA's naming standards.  That said, I can see how the message
is confusing.  Is it inaccurate to say that the identifier is
unavailable when it doesn't meet standards?  Do we need two different
messages perhaps (ie, "not available" and "identifiers can not contain
...")?
